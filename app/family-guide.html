What each number means

Each row maps a number on the NVDA card above to a plain-English explanation.

Card element What it means (NVDA example)

Section 3

What the four signals mean

The coloured bar at the top of each card and the signal label are both driven by the composite score (0–100).

🟢 STRONG BUY
Score ≥ 65. Multiple strong signals aligned — momentum, analysts, and value all pointing up. Highest conviction.
🟡 BUY
Score 50–64. Solid setup with most signals positive. Worth watching closely and doing more research.
⚪ WATCH
Score 35–49. Mixed signals. Not a clear buy yet — add to watchlist and revisit the next day's run.
🔴 AVOID
Score < 35. Weak or deteriorating signals across multiple layers. Not the right time — don't chase it.
Section 4

Why there are two runs each weekday

The engine runs automatically every weekday morning Sydney time. The dashboard shows a separate tab for each run so you can compare how signals shifted overnight vs after market open.

⏰ 5am — Early Signal

Runs before the US market opens. Uses overnight data, pre-market moves, and the latest analyst revisions. Good for spotting overnight shifts before markets react.

🔔 7am — Final Signal

Runs after the US market open. Incorporates the first hour of live trading. More data = higher confidence. This is the primary run to focus on.

Section 5

How to open the dashboard on your iPhone

1
Open Safari Must be Safari — not Chrome or Firefox — to add to Home Screen.
2
Go to the dashboard URL
karthikpalsg.github.io/stock-recommender/app/dashboard.html
3
First visit: enter your access token You'll see a "Connect GitHub" screen. Karthik will send you a personal token — paste it in once and tap Save. It stays saved on your device permanently.
4
Add to Home Screen (optional but recommended) Tap the Share icon (box with arrow pointing up) → "Add to Home Screen" → Add. You get a full-screen app icon — no browser chrome, no URL bar.
5
It updates itself The dashboard polls for new data every 5 minutes automatically. After each weekday morning run the cards refresh on their own — no need to reload.
